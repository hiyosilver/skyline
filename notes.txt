Feature list

Jobs
	- one can be run at a time
	- take a given amount of ticks
	- generate income at the end
	- can have fail chance each tick
	- can have crew slots
	- crew slots can be optional or required
	- required crew slots need to be filled for job to start
	- optional crew slots don't have to be filled but give bonuses
	- each crew slot has a type, depending on which crew member stat it uses
	- TODO: make non optional crew slots require a certain level of stats (currently no restriction at all)

Crew
	- the have a default job they do on their own, generating income
	- the get a salary at the end of each period
	- they leave if they aren't paid
	- the have stats: brawn, savvy, tech and charisma
	- TODO: make crew members hireable up to a limit
	- TODO: make crew members trainable, with a chance to increase certain stats

Heists
	- longer, more compliated jobs
	- 3 phases: planning, execution, finish

Buildings
	- have upgrades that can be bought that unlock bonuses and features
	- can be bought for a substantial amount and then generate income
	- have additional upgrades that become available after buying the property
	- TODO: previous upgrades are automatically unlocked when buying it

Stocks
	- TODO: should probably unlock through building upgrade or similar
	- interface with a list of available stocks, current price and price trend
	- ability to buy or sell stock at current price
	- stocks are not treated as individual lots but one large amount with an averaged cost basis

Bonds (TODO)
	- should be somewhat similar to bonds
	- not generally available but get issued by some companies or the government randomly
	- list of available bonds where player can chose to buy the issue
	- generates income on quarter or year update (maybe 2 - 4% base rate for gov, +2-10% extra risk premium for business) aka "coupon"
	- after the full time has elapsed (reached maturity), the principal is returned and the bond removed
	- when selling early, if current bond interestest is higher, the bond is worth less and can only be sold at a loss (and vice versa)
